Nutritional Wellness Tips for Students

Step 1: Balanced Diet

Eat plenty of colorful fruits and veggies.Choose whole grains like brown rice and whole wheat bread.

Include lean proteins like chicken, fish, or beans.Add healthy fats from foods like avocados and nuts.Avoid too many processed foods and sugary snacks.

Step 2:Regular Meals

Plan meals around your class schedule to ensure you have time to eat.

Pack portable snacks like trail mix, fruit, or granola bars to eat between classes.Utilize campus dining options or meal plans to access affordable and convenient meals

Step 3: Hydration

Carry a reusable water bottle with you on campus and refill it throughout the day.Take advantage of water fountains or refill stations to stay hydrated between classes.Limit sugary drinks like soda and energy drinks, opting for water or unsweetened beverages instead.

Step 4: Include Healthy Snacks

Keep healthy snacks in your backpack or dorm room for quick energy boosts.Choose budget-friendly options like peanut butter on whole grain crackers, yogurt, or carrot sticks with hummus.Avoid vending machine snacks and fast food, which can be expensive and lacking in nutrition.

Avoid unhealthy snacks high in sugar, salt, and saturated fats.

Step 5: Portion Control

Use portion control containers or utensils to help you gauge appropriate serving sizes.Practice mindful eating by paying attention to your hunger and fullness cues.

Avoid going back for seconds unless you're truly hungry to prevent overeating

Step 6: Limit Processed Foods

Opt for whole food snacks like fruit, nuts, or popcorn instead of packaged snacks.Cook simple meals using fresh ingredients when possible, such as pasta with marinara sauce and vegetables.

Read food labels and choose minimally processed options with fewer additives and preservatives

Step 7: Meal Planning

Plan meals for the week ahead and make a grocery list to save time and money.

Look for budget-friendly recipes that are quick and easy to prepare, such as stir-fries or grain bowls.Cook in batches and freeze leftovers for future meals to reduce food waste.

Step 8: Mindful Eating

Take time to enjoy your meals without distractions like studying or watching TV.Chew your food slowly and savor the flavors to enhance satisfaction.

Listen to your body's hunger and fullness signals to avoid overeating.
